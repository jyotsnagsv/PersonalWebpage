Open Quantum Systems

I am a PhD student at UC San Diego working with Prof. Daniel Arovas on open quantum systems and non-equilibrium steady states, with a focus on the effects of dissipation and dephasing. My work involves formulating the effects of the environment on many-body fermionic quantum systems and understanding how the mixed states thereby obtained can be studied to understand phase transitions and time scales involved in these systems. I am on the job market for postdoctoral positions starting June 2027.

I completed my bachelor's and master's at IISc Bengaluru. For my theses, I worked on many-body localization (bachelor's) and Josephson Junction arrays (master's) with Prof. Subroto Mukerjee and Prof. H R Krishnamurthy.

During summer internships, I have also worked on experimental studies of topological hall effect and magnetoresistance in thin films.
