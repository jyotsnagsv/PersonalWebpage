Dissipative Dirac matrix spin model in two dimensions

Jyotsna Gidugu, Daniel P. Arovas

Physical Review A 109, 022212 (2024)

We studied the generalization of a dissipative spin chain to two dimensions which was equivalent to a non-Hermitian Kitaev solvable model. We used third quantization to study the degrees of freedom to understand the spectrum and the non equilibrium steady states. We also used computational methods such as simulated annealing to estimate the Liouvillian gap in large systems. We also described a perturbative approach to study the Liouvillian gap for strong and weak dissipation.

Transport in the nonergodic extended phase of interacting quasiperiodic system

Soumi Ghosh, Jyotsna Gidugu, Subroto Mukerjee

Physical Review B, 102, 224203 (2020)

We studied the transport properties in the nonergodic extended phase of an interacting spinless fermion system in a quasiperiodic potential. To identify the phase, we used diagnostics such as spectral statistics, Renyi entropy and ETH violation. We then studied dc conductivity in this phase and found signatures of subdiffusive transport.

Topological Hall Signatures of Two Chiral Spin Textures Hosted in a Single Tetragonal Inverse Heusler Thin Film

Pranava K. Sivakumar, B¨orge G¨obel, Edouard Lesne, Anastasios Markou, Jyotsna Gidugu, James M. Taylor, Hakan Deniz, Jagannath Jena, Claudia Felser, Ingrid Mertig, and Stuart S. P. Parkin

ACS Nano, 14, 13463-13469 (2020)

We studied the topological Hall effect (THE) in Heusler thin films of Mn2RhSn to understand the conditions (e.g. magnetic field and temperature) that favor skyrmions and antiskyrmions. The contribution of THE to the total Hall resistivity was identified. The study of the magnitude of THE under different conditions helped us understand the spin alignments in the material and how they affected the transport properties.

Strain induced ferromagnetism and large magnetoresistance of epitaxial La1.5Sr0.5CoMMnO6 thin films.

J Krishna Murthy, G Jyotsna, Nileena N, P S Anil Kumar.

Journal of Applied Physics, 122, 065307 (2017)

We prepared thin films of La1.5Sr0.5CoMMnO6 (LSCMO) using pulsed laser deposition and studied their structural properties using X-ray diffraction. We studied the magnetic phase transition temeperatures and the M-H hysteresis loops of LSCMO. The transport properties (as a function of temperature and magnetic field) were also studied to understand the mechanism behind the magnetoresistance.
